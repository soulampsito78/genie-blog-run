## 3. Prototype publication pattern

The Kee-Suri prototypes repeated a **publication logic** stronger than a news digest. Below, each block maps prototype behavior to official contract intent.

### A. Opening signal

**Prototype behavior:**

- Opened with **current time/date** or immediate briefing entry — “this is today’s read.”
- Created **currentness and urgency** — the briefing matters *now*, not as evergreen reference.
- Established Kee-Suri as a **tech briefing persona** delivering filtered signal.
- Early prototypes sometimes used **테크 앵커** framing — product logic was strong; identity label was wrong.

**Contract implication:**

- Opening should create **currentness pressure** and **why today matters**.
- Adapt persona to locked identity: **테크 비서 키수리** / **private tech secretary** — not 테크 앵커.
- Opening should **not** be greeting-first or weather-comfort-first.

### B. Fresh news cluster

**Prototype behavior:**

- Presented **3 major items** in prototypes (high signal density).
- Official schema uses **TOP 5** — prototypes’ “three strong signals” logic scales to five slots without changing rhythm.
- Each item treated as a **signal**, not a republished article.
- Headlines often followed: **entity + action + consequence** (who did what, and why it moves the board).

**Contract implication:**

- TOP 5 items are **signal surfaces** with `why_it_matters` and `business_implication` carrying prototype’s “consequence” energy.
- Title structure per item: company/platform/policy actor + move + downstream pressure.

### C. Structural interpretation — **키수리의 딥-다이브**

**Prototype behavior:**

- **Did not repeat** the news list.
- **Grouped separate stories** into one larger structural movement.
- Example movement themes observed in prototypes:
  - AI infrastructure expansion
  - Supply-chain control
  - AI sovereignty
  - Physical infrastructure shift
  - Security / control paradigm shift
  - Platform power movement

**Contract implication:**

- **키수리의 딥-다이브** answers: “What is *actually* moving when you put today’s signals together?”
- Use `key_implications` bullets for secondary pressures inside the movement.
- Forbidden: restating each TOP item in prose; generic **심층 분석** label.

### D. Side-effect detection

**Prototype behavior:**

- Asked **what changes because of the news** — not only what happened.
- Detected **secondary consequences**, e.g.:
  - Market-cap shock
  - Regulation / sovereignty pressure
  - Hardware bottlenecks
  - Infrastructure capital concentration
  - Vendor lock-in
  - National bloc formation
  - Entry barriers
  - Workflow / control shift

**Contract implication:**

- Side-effect reading belongs primarily in **키수리의 딥-다이브** body and implications — optionally echoed in TOP `risk_note`.
- This is a **product differentiator** — preserve aggressively in the contract.

### E. Directional judgment

**Prototype behavior:**

- Gave a **clear reading** of where the market/industry is moving.
- Avoided useless neutrality — still **useful** to a CEO/operator reader.
- Stopped short of **investment advice** — strategic direction, not trade tickets.

**Contract implication:**

- Direction belongs in **키수리의 딥-다이브** and compresses into **원-라인 체크포인트**.
- Tone: bold, controlled, premium analyst — not hype, not disclaimer soup.

### F. One-line compression — **원-라인 체크포인트**

**Prototype behavior:**

- Ended analytical arc with **one decisive sentence**.
- Function was **decision cue**, not recap.
- Prototypes sometimes drifted spelling (“한 줄 체크포인트”) — official label is **`원-라인 체크포인트`** only.

**Contract implication:**

- **원-라인 체크포인트** = what to watch, who gains control, where pressure moves next.
- Forbidden: soft summary, vague lesson, **핵심 요약**, 원포인트 / One-Point variants.

### G. Image → body → checkpoint rhythm

**Prototype behavior:**

- **Top image** opened authority and currentness.
- **Body** carried signal → structure → side effects → direction.
- **Second / bottom image** (domestic evening prototypes) led into final checkpoint and emotional close — reader felt the day **ended** with Kee-Suri.

**Contract implication:**

- 12:30 global: top-shot + body sections — **no** bottom-shot close block.
- 18:30 Korea: top-shot + body + **promoted** bottom-shot (future) + warm text close (§11) + **마무리 및 출처 리스트**.

### H. Domestic emotional closing (future — promotion-gated)

When a **production-promoted** domestic bottom-shot is available, add a **warm closing text section below the bottom-shot image**:

**Tone:** warm, controlled, premium, private-secretary-like — not girlfriend, not weathercaster comfort, not overly sentimental.

## 7. Official logical flow to preserve

Reusable Kee-Suri analytical arc for title/body contract:

```
Step 1: What changed today?
        → 글로벌/국내 테크 TOP 5

Step 2: Why is this not just news?
        → transition into 키수리의 딥-다이브

Step 3: What structure is moving?
        → deep_dive.body — movement thesis

Step 4: What second-order effects appear?
        → deep_dive.body + key_implications

Step 5: Who gains control / who loses leverage?
        → deep_dive conclusion thread

Step 6: What should the reader watch, hold, or prepare for?
        → bridges to 원-라인 체크포인트

Step 7: 원-라인 체크포인트
        → one decisive direction cue (not recap)

Step 8: [18:30 Korea only, bottom-shot production-promoted]
        → warm farewell below bottom-shot (§11)

Step 9: 마무리 및 출처 리스트
        → closing_message + source_list
```

## 8. Title / opening implication

**Do not write the final title contract in this document.** Extract rules only:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Title exposes **structural movement** | Not only the single biggest headline |
| Opening is **not greeting-first** | No “안녕하세요” lead dominance |
| Opening creates **currentness pressure** | Why this briefing matters **today** |
| Opening states **stakes** | What shifted in the landscape since last read |
| Title/lead compatible with **TOP 5 + 키수리의 딥-다이브** | Lead must not duplicate entire TOP list |
| Identity | **테크 비서 키수리** — never 테크 앵커 in customer copy |

**Opening power (Opal recovery):** Subject/lead quality without renaming locked section headings.

## 9. Directional judgment rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Be bold about direction | Reader should feel oriented, not merely informed |
| Do not overclaim certainty | No false precision on fast-moving tech news |
| No investment advice | No buy/sell/hold tickets; no price targets as instruction |
| Preferred phrasing (Korean) | 가능성이 커진다 · 압력이 생긴다 · 이동하고 있다 · 진입 장벽이 높아진다 · 통제권이 이동한다 |
| Forbidden phrasing | 반드시 오른다 · 확정 수익 · 대박 · 무조건 · guaranteed upside |
| Placement | Primary: **키수리의 딥-다이브**; compressed: **원-라인 체크포인트** |

## 10. Side-effect detection rules

Side-effect categories to preserve in **키수리의 딥-다이브** (and optionally `risk_note` on TOP items):

| Category | Example read |
|----------|--------------|
| Market shock | Valuation / cap-table pressure from a single event |
| Infrastructure bottleneck | Compute, power, fab, datacenter constraints |
| Regulation / sovereignty | National AI rules, export controls, data residency |
| Platform lock-in | Ecosystem capture deepens |
| Supply-chain control | Chip, cloud, or model supply concentration |
| Data / security control | Access, audit, compliance leverage shifts |
| Labor / workflow shift | Who automates what; control of knowledge work |
| Startup entry barrier | Capital, distribution, or regulation raises floor |
| Capital concentration | Big-tech / hyperscaler spend pulls industry gravity |
| National bloc formation | US/EU/China/Korea alignment or decoupling moves |

**Method:** For each TOP signal, ask “If this is true, what **else** must change?” — that answer feeds the movement thesis.
